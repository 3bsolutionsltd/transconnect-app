# Onboarding Guide: Brand Ambassador

**Last Updated:** July 2, 2026  
**Version:** 1.0  
**Target Role:** Brand Ambassador ("The Infantry")

## Overview

Welcome to TransConnect! As a Brand Ambassador, you are the first point of contact for thousands of passengers discovering our platform. This guide will prepare you to confidently engage with passengers, demonstrate the app, achieve your daily targets, and represent TransConnect with professionalism and enthusiasm during your first week.

**Your Mission:** Convert terminal foot traffic into registered users and first-time bookers while educating passengers and collecting valuable feedback to improve the platform.

### Day 3 (Thursday, July 3rd) - Customer Engagement & Sales Techniques

#### Morning Session (9:00 AM - 12:00 PM)
- ✅ **9:00 AM**: Customer approach strategies:
  - **Reading body language**: Who's open to conversation vs. who's rushing
  - **Opening lines**: "Good morning! Have you heard about TransConnect?"
  - **Permission-based approach**: "May I show you something that'll save you time?"
  - **Non-intrusive positioning**: Near queues, not blocking pathways
- ✅ **9:45 AM**: The 4-step engagement process:
  1. **Greet warmly** → Smile, eye contact, friendly tone
  2. **Identify need** → "Are you traveling soon?" "Tired of long queues?"
  3. **Demonstrate value** → Quick app demo showing seat selection/payment
  4. **Guide action** → Help them download, register, or complete first booking
- ✅ **10:30 AM**: Handling different passenger types:
  - **Tech-savvy youth**: Quick demo, emphasize convenience
  - **First-time app users**: Patient walkthrough, stay with them
  - **Elderly passengers**: Extra patience, larger text, simpler language
  - **Skeptical passengers**: Social proof, show branded buses, security features
  - **Busy professionals**: Highlight time-saving benefits
- ✅ **11:15 AM**: Objection handling scripts:
  - Listen fully before responding
  - Acknowledge concerns with empathy
  - Provide clear, truthful answers
  - Offer to help them try it risk-free

#### Afternoon Session (2:00 PM - 5:00 PM)
- ✅ **2:00 PM**: Marketing materials distribution:
  - When to hand out flyers (always after a conversation, not randomly)
  - Sticker placement on buses (with operator permission only)
  - How to use visual aids to explain features
  - Tracking daily distribution (count remaining at end of day)
- ✅ **2:45 PM**: Feedback collection techniques:
  - 5+ qualitative insights daily across three categories:
    - **Usability**: Is the app easy to use? Any confusing parts?
    - **Payment**: Any issues with mobile money? Preferred payment methods?
    - **Value**: Do they see the benefit? What would make them use it more?
  - How to ask open-ended questions
  - Writing clear, actionable feedback notes
- ✅ **3:30 PM**: Safety and conflict de-escalation:
  - Never argue with passengers
  - If someone is hostile, politely excuse yourself
  - Report any threats or harassment to Field Operations Lead immediately
  - Stay aware of your surroundings
  - Work in pairs during early morning/late evening if possible
  - Emergency contact protocol
- ✅ **4:00 PM**: Full role-play simulation:
  - Field Operations Lead acts as different passenger types
  - Practice complete interactions from greeting to booking
  - Get feedback on tone, body language, messaging
  - Refine your personal approach

## Pro Tips for Success

### Getting to Target Numbers

**For Bookings (15 per day):**
- Focus on passengers actively heading to ticket counters
- Catch them BEFORE they buy physical tickets
- Offer to help complete their first booking right there
- Stay with them through payment if needed
- Peak hours are your best time—be energized!

**For Installations (10 per day):**
- Target passengers waiting for buses (they have time)
- Show how easy it is: "Takes just 2 minutes"
- Do it together on their phone, not just yours
- Ensure they complete registration before moving on
- Follow up: "Book your next trip before you leave!"

**For Flyer Distribution (500+ per day):**
- Always give flyers AFTER a conversation, not randomly
- Hand them to every passenger you interact with
- Leave stacks at visible spots with permission (info desks, waiting areas)
- Give multiple flyers to interested people ("Share with friends!")
- Track by counting remaining flyers at day's end

**For Feedback Collection (5+ per day):**
- Ask open-ended questions: "What do you think?" "How was that?"
- Listen for specific details: "The payment step was confusing"
- Write it down immediately while fresh
- Mix of positive and negative feedback is valuable
- Real quotes are powerful: Use their exact words

### Making Every Interaction Count

1. **Energy is contagious**: Your enthusiasm makes passengers curious
2. **Smile genuinely**: People respond to warmth
3. **Be helpful, not salesy**: Focus on solving their problem
4. **Stay patient**: Especially with first-time app users
5. **Celebrate small wins**: Every installation is progress!
6. **Learn continuously**: What worked? What didn't? Adjust.

### Building Your Personal Approach

- **Find your style**: Professional but authentic to who you are
- **Test different openers**: See what resonates best
- **Share tips with teammates**: Collaboration over competition
- **Stay consistent**: Show up, do the work, results will come
- **Track your progress**: Watch yourself improve week by week

## Common Challenges & Solutions

### Challenge 1: "Passengers are ignoring me"
**Solutions:**
- Improve your positioning (stand where they naturally slow down)
- Soften your approach (less "salesy," more "helpful")
- Make eye contact and smile FIRST, then speak
- Try different opening lines
- Target people waiting/sitting, not rushing

### Challenge 2: "I'm not hitting my numbers"
**Solutions:**
- Focus on peak hours (6-10 AM, 4-8 PM)
- Increase your interaction volume (talk to more people)
- Improve your demo speed (faster = more opportunities)
- Ask Field Operations Lead for shadowing/coaching
- Review what top performers are doing differently

### Challenge 3: "People say they'll download later (but won't)"
**Solutions:**
- Do it together NOW: "Let me help you download real quick"
- Remove friction: Use their phone, you guide
- Create urgency: "First-time users get a discount today"
- Make it fun: "Let's see how easy this is!"

### Challenge 4: "Technical issues during demos"
**Solutions:**
- Always have your phone charged and ready as backup
- Know the troubleshooting steps (restart app, check internet)
- Collect their contact for follow-up if something's broken
- Report technical issues to Field Operations Lead immediately
- Stay calm and professional when tech fails

### Challenge 5: "Feeling discouraged after slow days"
**Solutions:**
- Remember: Every expert was once a beginner
- Talk to Field Operations Lead for encouragement
- Focus on quality interactions, not just numbers
- Celebrate non-number wins (great conversations, positive feedback)
- Tomorrow is a fresh start

## Safety & Professionalism Guidelines

### Personal Safety
- ✅ Stay in well-lit, populated areas of the terminal
- ✅ Keep your phone and valuables secure
- ✅ Work in pairs during early/late hours if possible
- ✅ Know where security personnel are located
- ✅ Trust your instincts—avoid uncomfortable situations
