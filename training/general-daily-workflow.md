# Daily Workflow: Field Operations Lead ("The General")

**Last Updated:** July 9, 2026  
**Version:** 1.0  
**Target Role:** Field Operations Lead ("The General")

## Overview

This guide defines a repeatable daily rhythm for the Field Operations Lead. The General ensures terminal readiness, field-team coordination, safety, and smooth operator workflows so passengers receive a consistent, reliable experience.

Follow the schedule below while remaining flexible for incidents and high-priority operational needs.

---

## Morning Brief (7:00 AM - 8:30 AM)

### 7:00 AM — Terminal Readiness Check
- [ ] Inspect key terminals (or receive morning checklist from terminal managers)
  - Confirm vehicle manifests, operator staffing, and terminal signage
  - Verify boarding queues are organized and signage visible
  - Ensure POS terminals and ticket printers are powered and connected

### 7:30 AM — Field Communications & Shift Handover
- [ ] Run a 10–15 minute check-in with on-duty Field Supervisors and Terminal Managers
  - Review today’s route schedule, expected peak windows, and resource gaps
  - Confirm assigned duties and escalation points for each shift
  - Receive overnight incident summaries (lost items, complaints, system alerts)

### 8:00 AM — Systems & Staffing Verification
- [ ] Confirm that drivers/attendants have required documents and IDs
- [ ] Verify that daily cash reconciliation sheets are available for cash-handling terminals
- [ ] Confirm mobile connectivity (SIMs / data) for field teams

---

## Peak Shift Management (8:30 AM - 11:30 AM)

### 8:30 AM — Boarding & Queue Flow
- [ ] Oversee boarding discipline and queue management on high-traffic routes
- [ ] Ensure validators/QR scanners are working and operators understand validation rules
- [ ] Move extra staff to bottleneck terminals proactively

### 9:30 AM — Field Support & Escalations
- [ ] Monitor live Slack/WhatsApp incident channel for critical items
  - Resolve payment disputes and boarding discrepancies in real-time
  - Triage technical issues (ticketing, scanning) with Technical Lead

### 10:30 AM — Performance Checkpoint
- [ ] Validate on-the-ground metrics: departures on-time, boarding times, ticketing errors
- [ ] Log any repeated operational friction to fix by afternoon

---

## Midday Operations & Coordination (11:30 AM - 2:00 PM)

### 11:30 AM — Resource Allocation
- [ ] Re-deploy field teams based on morning volume and forecasts
- [ ] Approve short-notice overtime and break coverage as needed

### 12:00 PM — Finance & Cash Controls
- [ ] Ensure cash cages and reconciliation are operating per policy
- [ ] Address any discrepancies immediately with terminal manager

### 12:30 PM — Stakeholder Check-ins
- [ ] Connect with partner operators to confirm route integrity and vehicle condition
- [ ] Confirm airport/major hub coordination if there are special services

---

## Afternoon Focus (2:00 PM - 6:00 PM)

### 2:00 PM — Field Coaching & Quality
- [ ] Conduct brief coaching sessions with staff who need improvement
- [ ] Spot-check validator accuracy and passenger-facing behaviours

### 3:30 PM — Incident Review and Follow-up
- [ ] Review unresolved incidents from morning and midday
- [ ] Escalate unresolved technical bugs to Engineering with clear reproduction steps

### 4:30 PM — Peak-Eve Preparation
- [ ] Prepare extra staff for evening peak (5:00–8:00 PM)
- [ ] Confirm spare validators, chargers, and printed tickets available

---

## End-of-Day (6:00 PM - 7:30 PM)

### 6:00 PM — Reconciliation and Reporting
- [ ] Ensure cash reconciliations are complete and uploaded to shared drive
- [ ] Review daily field KPI dashboard and note anomalies

### 6:30 PM — Handover & Night Coverage
- [ ] Handover to night shift or on-call supervisor with incident log and priorities
- [ ] Confirm night escalation contacts (technical, legal, safety)

### 7:00 PM — Team Closeout
- [ ] Quick debrief with Field Supervisors: wins, learnings, outstanding items
- [ ] Confirm next day’s critical staffing and supplies (e.g., ticket rolls)

---

## Recurring Weekly Tasks

- Monday: Review weekend performance, set week priorities, update route allocations
- Tuesday: Vehicle & terminal maintenance scheduling review
- Wednesday: Training session / role-play for customer interactions and conflict resolution
- Thursday: Deep-dive on complaints and root-cause analysis of recurring issues
- Friday: Weekly report assembly and stakeholder briefing (operations team + leadership)
- Monthly: Safety & compliance audit with incident trend analysis

## Core Responsibilities (summary)

- Terminal readiness and queue management
- Field-team scheduling, coaching, and performance reviews
- Incident triage (safety, payments, ticketing, customer complaints)
- Cash control & reconciliation oversight
- Coordination with Technical, Marketing, and Partnerships teams
- On-the-ground implementation of promotions and campaigns

## Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

| KPI | Target | Actions that drive it |
|-----|--------|---------------------|
| On-time departures | > 95% | Shift planning, buffer vehicles, proactive re-deployment |
| Boarding time per passenger | < 45s | Queue discipline, additional check-in staff |
| Validator uptime | 99% | Spare devices, chargers, remote monitoring |
| Cash reconciliation accuracy | 100% | Strict cash controls, dual sign-off |
| Incidents resolved same day | > 90% | Clear escalation, morning triage, accountability |

## Emergency Protocols

### Critical (Call immediately)
- Safety incident with injuries, vehicle accidents, or threats to staff/passengers
- Data breach or payment-system outage causing revenue loss

Action: Call Technical Lead + Ops Director; send incident message in incident channel; secure affected terminals.

### High (Within 1 hour)
- Repeated payment failures on a route
- Large-scale customer complaints or social media escalation

Action: Triage, temporary mitigation (manual tickets), escalate to Engineering/Marketing.

### Standard (Within 24 hours)
- Single-customer technical issue; missing route data; minor ticket printing errors

Action: Log in tracker, resolve or schedule fix, confirm with customer.
